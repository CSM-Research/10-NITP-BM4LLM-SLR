============================================================
              BM4LLM-SLR - EXPERIMENTAL PROTOCOL
============================================================

GOAL:
To evaluate the BM4LLM-SLR model using a curated benchmark dataset
and a reproducible procedure that balances LLM automation and 
human intervention in the study selection stage of SLRs.

============================================================
1. BENCHMARK DATA SELECTION
============================================================

 - Benchmark dataset: SESLR-Eval
 - Reason: Provides a curated list of peer-reviewed SLRs
   mined from replication packages of high-quality reviews
   in Software Engineering.
 - Secondary studies selected: 6 (from SESLR-Eval)
   References:
     Stradowski 2023
     Alonso 2023
     Somers 2023
     Lewowski 2022
     Zakeri Nasrabadi 2023
     Sharbaf 2022
 - Ground Truth:
   Decisions taken by the original SLR authors
   after human scrutiny of primary studies.

============================================================
2. LLM RESOURCES AND EXPERIMENTAL SETUP
============================================================

 - Selected free-access LLMs:
     * GPT-4
     * Gemini 2.5 Flash
     * DeepSeek
     * Claude 4 Sonnet
 - Rationale:
   Enables replication of the experiment while 
   minimizing economic barriers to using LLMs.

============================================================
3. STUDY SAMPLING AND AUTOMATION TOOL
============================================================

 - Subsets of 20 primary studies were randomly selected
   for evaluation within each secondary study.
 - Total of 6 secondary studies evaluated.
 - Purpose:
   Reduce bias from single-SLR evaluation.

 - A tool was developed to automate:
     * Random selection
     * Prompt insertion into LLMs
     * Response collection
     * Classification storage
   (Repository: https://github.com/CSM-Research/10-BM4LLM-SLR)

 - Decision threshold:
     * Acceptable scores = 6 (agree) or 7 (strongly agree)
     * Scale: 1–7 Likert
     * Classification used for inclusion or exclusion.

============================================================
4. PILOT TESTING AND SCREENING MODES
============================================================

 - Pilot testing was performed to refine:
     * Selection criteria
     * Output statements
     * LLM configurations
     * Noise removal from data

 - Number of studies submitted: 480

 - Automated decision threshold:
     * Score >= 6 to include or exclude a study

 - Screening modes:
     A. Low-Confidence Mode:
        - 240 studies
        - Decisions made solely by multiple LLMs
        - Aggregated via artificial consensus

     B. High-Confidence Mode:
        - 240 studies
        - Decisions made by two LLMs + one human researcher
        - Final decision authority: human

 - Assumptions:
     * Human intervention always yields the correct decision
     * Evaluation focuses only on LLM automatic decisions
       (not human error or social factors).

============================================================
5. RESULT SYNTHESIS AND METRICS
============================================================

 - Tool comparison:
   LLM results vs. original human decisions from the 
   secondary studies.

 - Performance metrics:
     * True Positives (TP)
     * False Positives (FP)
     * True Negatives (TN)
     * False Negatives (FN)

 - Objective:
   Minimize FN (false negatives) to reduce coverage bias.

 - Accuracy formula:
     Accuracy = (TP + TN) / (TP + TN + FP + FN)

 - Recall (Sensitivity) formula:
     Recall = TP / (TP + FN)

 - Interpretation:
     * Accuracy: overall correct classifications
     * Recall: model's ability to correctly include
       all relevant studies.

============================================================
6. ITERATIVE IMPROVEMENT
============================================================

 - Total iterations: 3
 - Iteration 1:
     * Refinement of inclusion and exclusion criteria
     * Prompt structure adjustments

 - Iteration 2:
     * Introduction of pilot testing phase
     * Dataset review to reduce misinterpretation by LLMs

 - Iteration 3:
     * Minor fine-tuning
     * Data collection for evaluation

 - Results and detailed analysis documented in:
   Section "Evaluation Results"
